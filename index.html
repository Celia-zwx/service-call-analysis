Maintenance Plan Offer

Long-term service agreements
✗ Needs Improvement

What happened: The maintenance plan was dropped in a single sentence as a warranty perk — no plan name, no pricing, no document shown, no enrollment offer. The customer had no way to evaluate or say yes to it.

What should have happened: The technician should have paused and presented a concrete maintenance plan — ideally pulling up or handing over a physical or on-screen document showing:

  • Plan tiers (e.g. Basic / Premium / Elite)
  • Annual or monthly pricing
  • What's included — tune-ups, priority scheduling, parts discounts, filter replacements
  • The warranty match benefit spelled out clearly in writing
  • A simple sign-up step: "I can get you enrolled right now, takes 30 seconds"

A physical brochure or a dedicated screen in the estimate app would have made this tangible and easy to say yes to — especially for a customer already writing a $1,000 deposit check.
